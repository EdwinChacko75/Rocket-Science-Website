What is Rocket Science?
Rocket science is the branch of aerospace engineering that studies rockets and the many variables that affect their performance. Today we will discuss rockets in extraterrestrial endeavours.
Newton's Third Law
Sir Issac Newton discovered that for every action force, there is an equal and opposite reaction force. The aforementioned is Newtonâ€™s third law of motion and is one of the fundamental principles dictating the success of rockets. On earth, there is a thick atmosphere that allows for a variety of different sources of lift. Hot air balloons, airfoils, jet engines, and even electricity are to name a few. In space, however, there are negligible amounts of gas particles restricting the use of traditional methods of flight. Rockets work in space since they don't need an atmosphere to function.
